Your registrant is not eligible for a .gov.uk domain name

Your registrant cannot get approval for a .gov.uk domain name because they are not from an eligible organisation.

Check which organisations are eligible
  • central government department or agency - unless you qualify for an exemption
  • non-departmental body - also known as an arm’s length body
  • parish or community council
  • town, county, borough, metropolitan or district council
  • fire service
  • neighbourhood or village council
  • combined or unitary authority
  • police and crime commissioner
  • joint authority
  • joint committee
  • organisation representing a group of public sector bodies
  • organisation representing a profession across public sector bodies
If you still think that your registrant is eligible to apply, you can email approvals@domains.gov.uk.

For more information on eligibility read the guidance to check if your registrant’s organisation can get a .gov.uk domain name.
